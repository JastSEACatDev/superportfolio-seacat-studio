Design Philosophy

Spaces, not structures

A building is a container for experience. I design the journey through a space — sightlines, reveals, circulation, compression and release — not just the walls that define it. The path matters as much as the destination.

Materials have meaning

Every surface communicates. Heavy stone at a base says permanence. Warm wood in an interior says refuge. I research real-world material language and translate it faithfully, even when the medium is blocks.

Reference-driven process

I pull original blueprints from university archives. I study period-correct window proportions. I research streetcar rail gauges. The work looks right because it is right — accuracy is the foundation that intuition builds on.

Scale is a design tool

I work at every scale simultaneously — from city-level urban planning down to 1/16th-block chiseled furniture. The macro and micro must agree. A citadel that looks grand from a distance but empty up close has failed.
